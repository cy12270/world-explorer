Luang Prabang, Laos

Recognised as a UNESCO World Heritage site, Luang Prabang is a beautifully preserved town that exudes a sense of peace and timelessness. Located at the confluence of the Mekong and Nam Khan rivers in the mountains of northern Laos, the city is a stunning mosaic of gleaming golden-roofed temples, traditional wooden houses, and remnants of French colonial architecture. The spiritual heart of Laos, its most moving spectacle is the daily morning alms-giving ceremony (Tak Bat), where hundreds of saffron-robed monks walk silently through the streets. Beyond the town, you can swim in the cascading turquoise pools of the stunning Kuang Si Falls, explore sacred caves, and enjoy the slow, meditative pace of life that makes Luang Prabang a truly restorative destination.
